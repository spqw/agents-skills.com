Agents Skills vs Keyboard Maestro (2026)

AI-powered automation that learns from you vs. the most powerful macro engine on macOS. Here's how they actually compare.

Updated February 2026 · 5 min read

Keyboard Maestro has been the go-to automation tool for Mac power users for over a decade. It can automate nearly anything on macOS through its deep macro system. Agents Skills takes a different approach entirely — instead of asking you to build automations, it uses AI to learn your workflows and automate them for you.

Both tools aim to eliminate repetitive work, but they do it in fundamentally different ways. This comparison breaks down the key differences so you can pick the right tool for how you actually work.

When to Choose Agents Skills

Best for: People who want automation without the work

Agents Skills is the right choice if you want to automate repetitive tasks but don't want to spend time building and maintaining macros. The AI watches your screen, detects repetitive patterns, and creates "skills" that handle them going forward.

  • You're not technical — no scripting, no trigger configuration, no action chaining. You just work normally.
  • You use Windows and macOS — Agents Skills works on both platforms, so you're not locked into one ecosystem.
  • Your workflows change often — the AI adapts to variations in file names, UI layouts, and data formats without breaking.
  • You don't know what to automate yet — Agents Skills identifies patterns you might not even realize are repetitive.

When to Choose Keyboard Maestro

Best for: Mac power users who love building macros

Keyboard Maestro is a mature, battle-tested tool that gives you granular control over every aspect of macOS automation. If you enjoy the process of building automations and want deep system integration, it's hard to beat.

  • You want precise control — Keyboard Maestro lets you define exact triggers, conditions, variables, and actions for complex workflows.
  • You're a Mac-only user — the deep macOS integration (AppleScript, system events, menu access) is unmatched.
  • You have specific, stable workflows — if your processes rarely change, hand-crafted macros are reliable and efficient.
  • You enjoy the tinkering — building macros can be satisfying, and the community shares thousands of ready-made ones.

The Verdict

Keyboard Maestro and Agents Skills solve the same problem — repetitive work — but for different kinds of people.

Keyboard Maestro is a powerful, proven tool that rewards investment. If you're willing to learn its macro system and you work exclusively on macOS, it gives you a level of control that few tools can match. The $36 price is also hard to argue with for the depth of functionality you get.

Agents Skills is for people who want the result without the process. You don't learn a macro language. You don't configure triggers. You just work, and the AI handles the rest. It also works on both Windows and macOS, which matters if you use multiple machines or switch platforms.

If you already know and love Keyboard Maestro, there may be no reason to switch. But if you've tried it and found the learning curve too steep, or you want automation that adapts without maintenance, Agents Skills is worth trying. The free trial means you can evaluate it with zero risk.
